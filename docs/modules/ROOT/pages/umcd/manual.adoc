In line with Strategic Plan 2022-27, Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL) is focusing on reducing direct material costs and enhancing operational efficiency through its turnaround initiative SP 2022-27. A pivotal aspect of this transformation effort involves sectorial consolidation of material procurement, aiming to leverage economies of scale and streamline processes. To support this consolidation, BHEL plans to implement the robust data management practices for managing codes across BHEL.

As is known the purpose of material codification was identification, classification/grouping of similar items together, and digitize the same for proper inventory analysis, reporting and control. The need to rationalize material codes as per the standard _12-digit alpha numeric codes_ has been an oft repeated initiative across the company to promote and strengthen optimum resource utilisation, economic buying, eliminating duplicity, unifying the material base, elimination of non-moving inventory etc. The _Central Material Code Allocation_ (CMCA) system for new code generation was initiated in 43^rd^ _Interplant Standards Committee_ (IPSC) meeting in October 2012 and followed up with communication reference AA:MM: MCR: dated 27.05.2016 from the office of Director, (E R&D). [#_Toc180407759 .anchor]####Definition of Code and Material Codification Objectives

Code is a particular form of identification or reference which serves the dual purpose of establishing in a systematic manner the complete identity of an individual product, bringing out at the same time its similarity with other products.

It may consist of a brief and systematic combination of letters, numerals and / or symbols.

A systematic code, used in conjunction with a key for decoding, can assist identification of a product and enable its characteristics to be known.

Material codification is the process of transformation of lengthy word descriptions into brief Numeric / Alpha-numeric codes with the following objectives:

=== Identification

Material codes precisely identify the complete parameters of materials in terms of specifications, sizes etc. and facilitate procurement, storing and issue of the right material for the intended application.

=== Classification

Material codes groups similar

* Systematic storing
* Classified information retrieval
* Standardization / Rationalization / Unification / Variety control

=== Digitization

Material codes facilitate in

* Proper Inventory Analysis / Reporting / Control
* Computerised Indenting / Procurement / Issue
* Computerised material accounting / Costing

== Background of Rationalisation & Codification Exercise

Standardization and codification are necessary to reduce inventory burden. The need to rationalize material codes as per the standard 12-digit alpha numeric codes, an oft repeated initiative across the company, was taken up with renewed rigor since December 2022 with the sole purpose of optimizing resource utilization, enabling economic buying, eliminating duplicity, ensuring proper inventory analysis, etc. Out of a total of 12 lakhs plus material codes across the company, 8 lakh inactive material codes have been parked aside (to be used only if routed through _Unified Material Code Cell_ (UMCC)) and a residual 4 lakh active material codes have been considered for rationalisation. Of these 12 lakh material codes, the UMCC has addressed around 1.1 lakh material codes relating to series 1, 2, & 5 (_Metals_, _Insulating Materials_ and _Non-Metals_), and toned down the same to 14,189 uniform material codes, this effort has enabled _Unified Procurement Cell_ (UPC) formed at Delhi under the aegis of CAPEX and _Sourcing Strategy & Policy_ (SS&P), to ensure that procurement of Steel starting Q1 of 2024-25 is made from the uniform material codes only. UMCC to continue to address any other issues in this series (1,2 & 5).

Encouraged by our initial success in raw material codification, we made a sincere effort to extend the codes to component level codification that includes the 6 (_Standard Tools_), 7 (Standard Components), 8 (Maintenance Spares), and 9 (Product Components) series codes. On directive from Director, E, R&D, we also forayed into 3 series that we reserved for _Capital Goods_ codification across the Company.

Two Cross Functional Teams were formed one for _Capital Goods_ codification and another on system improvements in UMCD portal on 08.04.2024 and 13.04.2024. Meetings were held and the CFT on _Capital Goods_ codification finalized on a structure for the same that was distributed to all the major units and subsequently to the other units. The CFT on system improvements in UMCD portal met at Trichy and evolved a system for populating the erstwhile CMCA (Central Material Code Allocation system), now UMCD (Unified Material Code Directory) with all the 1 – 9 series codes.

Codification is a fundamental process in materials management that involves assigning unique codes to items within an organization’s inventory. This systematic approach streamlines the identification, tracking, and management of materials, contributing to efficient operations. Codification is a foundational component of materials management, providing structure and organization to inventory-related activities. By assigning unique codes to materials and maintaining accurate records, organizations can streamline their processes, improve inventory control, and enhance overall efficiency. It’s a critical tool for cost management and ensuring that the right materials are available when needed.

== Definition of Terms

=== Standardisation

An activity giving solutions for repetitive application, to problems essentially in the spheres of science, technology and economics, aimed at the achievement of optimum degree of order in a given context. Generally, the activity consists of the processes of formulating, issuing and implementing standards.

Note: an important benefit of standardization is the improvement of the suitability of the goods and services for their intended purpose.

=== Standard

A standard is a generally agreed-upon technology, method or format for a given application. Official standards, also known as de jure standards, are overseen by one of a number of governing bodies that exist to promote their development and confirm their status.

=== Company Standard

A specification of a product or process to be repeatedly and consistently used in the company.

=== Consensus

General Acceptance implying the absence of strong opposition by an important part of the interests concerned to substantial issues.

=== Variety Control

The selection of the optimum number of sizes, other characteristics or types of a product, required to meet prevailing needs.

=== Interchangeability

The suitability of a product (products) to be used in place of another product (products) to fulfil the relevant requirements.

Note: The functional aspect of interchangeability is called “functional interchangeability” and the dimensional aspect, “dimensional interchangeability”.

== Principles of Standardisation

=== Principle 1

Standardization is essentially an act of simplification as a result of the conscious effort of society. It calls for a reduction in the number of some things. It not only results in a reduction of present complexity but aims at the prevention of unnecessary complexity in the future.

=== Principle 2

Standardization is a social as well as an economic activity and should be promoted by the mutual co-operation of all concerned. The establishment of a standard should be based on general consensus.

=== Principle 3

The mere publication of a standard is of little value unless it can be implemented. Implementation may necessitate sacrifices by the few for the benefit of the many.

=== Principle 4

The action to be taken in establishing standards is essentially one of the selections followed by fixing.

=== Principle 5

Standards should be reviewed at regular intervals and revised as necessary. The interval between revisions will depend on particular circumstances.

=== Principle 6

When performance and other characteristics of a product are specified, the specification must include a description of the methods and tests to be applied in order to determine whether or not a given article complies with the specification.

When sampling is to be adopted the method, and if necessary the size and frequency of the sampling should be specified.

=== Principle 7

The necessity for legal enforcement of national standards should deliberately be considered, having regard to the nature of the standard, the level of industrialization and the laws and conditions prevailing in the society for whom the standard has been prepared.

=== Efficiency Technique

==== PERT

==== Value Analysis

===== Value Analysis is ‘Analysis by function’. It is first necessary to identify and then to establish the value of the function by considering the lowest cost of performing that function reliably. It aims at the identification and removal of unnecessary costs, namely costs, which do not provide usefulness, life, quality, appearance or customer’s needs.

===== Value analysis is an organized effect directed at analysing the function of hardware, systems and methods with the purpose of achieving the required function at the lowest overall cost consistent with requirements for performance, reliability and maintainability.

===== Value is made up of “use value” – the qualities and properties which accomplish a service or use and “esteem value” – the properties or features which make it a desirable thing to own.

===== Value is the minimum amount of money which must be spent to create the appropriate use and esteem function.

==== Consultation and Experts

Technical papers presented in seminars, conventions etc. and articles regarding the current subjects published in the concerned journals and periodicals as well as consultation with experts will be very much helpful for standardization.

=== Variety Reduction Technique

==== Preferred Numbers

Several series based on the above principles been promulgated from time to time but those which have been accorded first place are the “Renard series of Preferred Number” so named after the French Engineer Colonel Charles Renard who first proposed their use. There are four of these series (R5, R10, R20 and R40) as well as an additional series R80 intended for special application where very small steps in size are needed.

It will be seen that all these series repeat themselves in successive powers of 10. The R5 gives 6 sizes from 1 to 10 inclusive, with appropriately 58% increase in size between steps. Similarly, the R10 gives 11 sizes with approximately 26% increase. The R20 gives 21 sizes with 12% increase. The R40 gives 41 sizes with 6% increase and the R80 gives 81 sizes with 3% increase.

Examples

R5 : 1.00, 1.6, 2.5, 4.0, 6.3, 10.0

R10 : 1.00, 1.25, 1.6, 2.0, 2.5, 3.15, 4.0, 5, 6.3, 8, 10

==== ABC Analysis

Grouping of high value items, medium value items and low value items will be helpful to find the area to be concentrated for applying different techniques of standardization.

==== Design Unification

Due to different collaborations, various types of designs may be prevailing though the functional requirement of the component is same. Unification of designs will lead to variety reduction and reduced cost.

==== Coding and Classification

Material cost in Indian industries is estimated to be about 65-70% of production cost. Through raw material codification inventory reduction can be achieved. Significant codification requires number of digits whereas in sequential codification the number of digits can be reduced to the extent possible.

BHEL adopts 12-digit numbering scheme for materials and 11-digit numbering scheme for the production drawings.

=== Statistical Techniques

Sometimes technical requirements of different components and the dimensional tolerances stipulated in the national standards may not be adequate to establish a company standard. Hence, statistical methods such as sampling inspection or standard deviation etc. may be helpful for establishing the company standards.

== Codification in BHEL

The existing 12-digit alpha-numeric significant codification scheme which helps in computerized accounting and controlling the variety has been implemented across BHEL. The Standards Development Group and Unified Material Code cell at Corporate R&D is the nodal agency for maintaining and allotting these codes. The reference to company standards significance can be built up in material code to connect specification/standard number to avoid duplicate codes for the same material under different national standards. This decreases variety and inventory. Meaningful Management Information Reports on inventory can be easily generated using significant codification.

== Work Flow to generate material code through UMCD

Check for duplication of specification which is available in UMCD / VSE. Check if all material code requests conform to Format 2 for new code and Format 1 for old code as illustrated above.

=== Duplicity with Corporate data

Case 1:

Specification: If available at Corporate already, notify to unit about the details of specifications and provide data to unit for reviewing and upgradation to Corporate.

Material codes: Corporate material codes will be allotted for available sizes. For new sizes not available at Corporate, new codes will be generated through UMCD.

Case 2:

Specification: If units found any differences among specifications, units to provide their findings on non-upgradation of specification. Those specifications will be retained at unit level.

Material codes: Unit level codes will be generated as per IPSC guidelines and will be uploaded to UMCD.

=== Duplicity with other Units data:

Case 1:

Specifications: If similar specifications used across units, notify to all concerned units about the details of specifications and provide data to units for reviewing and upgradation to Corporate. If units agree new Corporate specification has to be prepared and unit level specification has to be withdrawn in lieu of new Corporate Specification.

Material codes: Corporate material codes will be generated for all sizes through UMCD.

Case 2:

Specifications: If units found any differences among specifications, units to provide their findings on non-merger of specification. Those specifications will be retained at unit level.

Material codes: Unit level codes will be generated as per IPSC guidelines and will be uploaded to UMCD.

=== Unit specific

Specifications: If no similar specification used at Corporate or Units, these specifications will be retained at unit level.

Material codes: Unit level codes will be generated as per IPSC guidelines and will be uploaded to UMCD.

Corporate R&D will upload all the unified specifications and material codes to UMCD

[#_Toc180407818 .anchor]####Pyramidal Approach to Standardisation

image:media/image6.png[media/image6,width=601,height=451]

The *Pyramidal Approach to Standardisation* is a strategic framework that emphasizes a hierarchical structure for effective collaboration and development across various levels of an organization and its stakeholders. This approach can be visualized as a pyramid, with each layer representing a different level of collaboration and standardization, progressing from the grassroots to the international level.

=== Importance of the Pyramidal Approach

The Pyramidal Approach to Standardisation provides a structured methodology for organizations to enhance their operational effectiveness through collaboration at multiple levels. By building from the bottom up, organizations can ensure that foundational elements are robust before layering on additional complexities. This approach not only fosters alignment within an organization but also enhances its ability to respond to market demands and regulatory changes effectively.

=== Role of Company Level Standards and Standardization

The Role of Company Level Standards and Standardization is pivotal in enhancing organizational efficiency, product quality, and compliance with broader regulatory frameworks. According to the International Organization for Standardization (ISO), company standards serve as internal guidelines tailored specifically for an organization, covering a range of documents that extend beyond national or international standards. These standards aim to improve productivity by optimizing design, reducing product variety, enhancing quality, and lowering costs.

==== Importance of Company Standards

===== Tailored Application:

Company standards are essential because they address specific operational needs that national standards may not fully cover. While national standards provide a general framework, company standards allow organizations to adapt these guidelines to their unique processes and products, ensuring relevance and effectiveness in application.

===== Implementation and Benefits:

The successful implementation of company standards is critical for realizing their benefits. Although the advantages of standardization may not be immediately apparent, they often lead to gradual improvements in efficiency and quality as associated technologies mature. Organizations are encouraged to continuously develop new standards without waiting for the complete evaluation of existing ones.

===== Profitability Enhancement:

One of the primary objectives of company-level standardization is to promote profitability through:

* *Standardization of Internal Processes:* This includes products, components, and methods that enhance coordination across operations.
* *Standardization of Goods and Services:* Achieving consistency in quality and compliance with international procedures can lead to cost reductions.
* *Harmonization of Customer Requirements:* Aligning customer expectations with regulatory demands minimizes product variety, streamlining offerings.

===== Comprehensive Coverage:

Company standards encompass a wide array of organizational activities including project design, quality checks, operational procedures, marketing, and personnel management. This holistic approach ensures that all processes operate under a well-defined system that promotes efficiency and accountability.

===== Interaction with National Standards:

Implementing company standards necessitates close collaboration with national standard bodies. This interaction not only facilitates the adoption of relevant national standards but also fosters a culture of standardization within the organization, driving continuous improvement.

[#_Toc180407884 .anchor]####Significant Features

The Significant features of the 12-digit material codification scheme are as follows:

* The scheme has flexibility for operation at Corporate/ Plant / Product levels.
* To collaborate further, materials rationalized at corporate level can be covered by corporate codes, exclusive plant requirements by plant codes and special requirement of Products such as Castings, Forgings, Sub-deliveries, and Styled Components etc. by Product codes; all in a single scheme. The Alphabetical prefixes and blocking in the rest of the digits facilitate this flexibility.
* The Classification is tailored to the needs of BHEL without sacrificing the generic nature, and facilitates retrieval of information to suit corporate Management Information Reporting.
* The material code numbers, Standard Numbers and Specification numbers are integrated for easy identification / communication / control.
* The check digit ensures correctness of the codes while inputting to the computerised systems.

[#_Toc180407890 .anchor]####Material Codification

=== Basic Guidelines for Codification

[arabic]
. The Interplant Standardization Committee (IPSC) has set the following guidelines which have been followed by the Unified Material Code Directory (UMCD) for codification of Metals (Carbon Steel, Alloy Steel, Non-Ferrous Metals), Insulating Materials, Non-Metals, Standard Tools, Standard Items while allotting the code numbers.

[arabic, start=7]
. Metric standard sizes (as far as possible to Indian Standards) only shall be allotted Corporate Code numbers. But in the case of tubes/pipes, inch/schedule sizes shall also be codified as it is not possible to avoid the use of this category of tubes / pipes.
. When a material is being used by more than one Unit, the same shall be identified for codification at corporate level.
. Non-standard sizes including inch sizes shall be operated at the plant level only with a long term plan to switch over to Corporate except in the case of tubes/ pipes as stated in clause 2 above.
. In the case of steel sheets, plates, shims, etc. Corporat Code Numbers shall be allotted on the basis of thickness only, irrespective of the width and length. However, coil form shall get a separate code number.
. Straight lengths and coils of the same materials will be covered under one specification only and the differentiation shall be made by suitable size codes.
. Corporate level specification / standard numbers shall be operated from 01-60 only (except Carbon Steel & Anti Friction Bearings) with the code '00' reserved for commercial / off-grade quality materials.
. Plant level specification / standards shall be operated from 99-61 (except Carbon Steel & Anti Friction Bearings) in the descending order prefixed with the plants 'alphabetical code’.
. Corporate level size codes shall be operated from 01-60 (except Carbon Steel & Anti Friction Bearings).
. Size codes 61-99 (except Carbon Steel & Anti Friction Bearings) shall be reserved for plant level operation with the plant prefix and with the corporate specification / standard number. These plant code numbers shall be operated in the descending order i.e. from 99-61 and NOT from 61-99.
. When a plant has consumed their allotted plant codes of "99-61", against a particular Corporate Standard and require further Plant codes, fold over of the Corporate Standard number, shall be resorted to. The fold over number shall be allotted only from the blank numbers.
. A one page Corporate document of the fold over Standard No. will be issued which would cross-refer to the original Corporate Standard No. for technical requirements and also state that, "Corporate Stndard Number is being issued only for codification".
. Only Standard Metric preferred sizes, as laid down in the respective Indian Standard, shall be codified.
. Whenever a Plant code number is lifted to Corporate level or Withdrawn / Cancelled / Superseded, this already allotted plant code shall be freezed & not reallotted / reused for a different size, at a later date.
. It shall be ensured that the persons allotting the Code Nos. in the various units are completely conversant with the guidelines for codification.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Carbon Steels

[arabic]
. Corporate level specification / standard numbers shall be operated from 01-80 only with the code '00' reserved for commercial / off-grade quality materials.
. Plant level specification / standards shall be operated from 99-81 in the descending order prefixed with the plants 'alphabetical code’.
. Corporate level size codes shall be operated from 01-80.
. Size codes 81-99 shall be reserved for plant level operation with the plant prefix and with the corporate specification / standard number. These plant code numbers shall be operated in the descending order i.e. from 99-81 and NOT from 81-99.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Alloy Steels

[arabic]
. In the case of items like electrical steel sheets, the width, length and insulation, if any shall also be taken into account while allotting corporate code numbers, if they are so specified in the corporate specification. If specification covers insulated and un-insulated, block could be allotted within the code barrel.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Non-Ferrous Metals

[arabic]
. Non-standard sizes shall be operated at the Plant level to facilitate to switch over to standard metric size / corporate code numbers in shape barrel 98 to 99.
. The tubes shall be codified on the basis of outer diameter and thickness only irrespective of length. The tubes procured in fixed lengths shall not be covered at Corporate level.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Insulating Materials, Insulated Conductors and Cables

[arabic]
. In the case of paper, sheets, boards, etc. code numbers shall be allotted on the basis of thickness only. irrespective of the width and length. However, code numbers for fixed length x width x thickness combination may also be codified as a special requirement.
. The tubes shall be codified on the basis of nominal bore (N.B.) x thickness only irrespective of length. Tubes procured in specified cut lengths shall not be covered at Corporate level.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Non-Metals

[arabic]
. In the case of paper, film, sheets, boards, etc. code numbers shall be allotted on the basis of thickness, thickness X width and / or thickness X width X length depending on the requirements.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Standard Tools

[arabic]
. Material code numbers for Standard Tools shall be allotted under the Primary category 6.
. Standard Tools conforming to National Standards / Leading suppliers catalogue and available off the shelf from reputed manufacturers shall be considered for codification.
. The method of indicating sizes shall be as given in Indian Standards to facilitate procurement.
. It has been decided to include accessories and spares exclusively used for a particular cutting tool under the same sub-group to which the concerned tool belong to. The type barrel of 6th and 7th digit in this case will be designated with the numerals 99.

Example:

+

+

. 622 XX XX XX indicates Files
+

+
622 99 01 XX indicates File Handles

[arabic, start=5]
. Specific Guidelines (Group - wise)

[loweralpha]
. Clamping & Holding Tools (Group 611): T-Bolts which are not exactly tools, shall not be codified under standard Tools (Primary category 6).
. Hand Tools (Group 614) - Spanners: Upto size 46 mm double ended open jaw spanners shall be codified. Above 46 mm single ended open jaw spanners shall be given code numbers.
. Portable Tools (group 681): Portable powered tools like grinders, drills, chippers shall be codified under Primary category 6. The spares for these items shall be codified under Primary category 8 by the Plants.
. Major Machine Tool Accessories like, Machine Vices, Rotary Tables, Indexing Head, Taper turning attachments, Lathe chucks, magnetic chucks, turret head accessories, etc. are being dealt by Maintenance, Technology & Services Dept. in most of the Units and therefore do not come under the purview of this group. Also these are durable items and do not come under stores inventory. These shall be codified under Primary category 8.

[arabic, start=6]
. Items like Tongs, Pokers, Spades, Shovels, Carpentry Tools, Blow Pipes, Welding accessories etc., which are special trade items and shall not be codified under Primary category 6.
. Coated abrasives like Emery cloth / Paper, Belts etc., and items like rotary wire brush, polishing mops, Lapping compound etc., which are consumable items and shall not be codified under Primary category 6.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Fasteners

[arabic]
. Material Code Numbers for Fasteners shall be allotted under the main Group 71.
. Fasteners conforming to National Standards / leading supplier's catalogue and available off the shelf from reputed manufacturers shall be considered for codification.
. Items such as eye bolts, plugs etc. shall not be covered under fasteners (Group-71) but shall be codified under ‘Standard Mechanical Components’ under Group - 724.
. The method of indicating sizes shall be as given in Indian Standards to facilitate procurement.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Antifriction Bearings

[arabic]
. Material Code Numbers for Antifriction bearings shall be allotted under the main group 721. This group covers ball and roller bearings and also other types of bearings covered by specific standard series of reputed manufacturers.
. Coding structure has been developed to identify each bearing on the basis of its boundary dimensions only and not taking into consideration variations in the design of bearing of different manufactures.
. The code structure will cover all bearings used for production as well as for maintenance. Accessories of bearings like balls, rollers, adapter sleeves, withdrawal sleeves, etc. are also codified.
. Metric bearings only are codified at corporate level. However, provision has been made in the coding Structure for inch bearings to facilitate plant level operation.
. Corporate level size cods for metric bearings shall be operated from 01 to 80 and plant cods from 99 to 81 in descending order.
. The code scheme shall cover normal clearance and zero precision bearings in a significant manner. Other grades and precision bearings are to be covered sequentially at the plant level only.
. In addition to the codification of basic bearings, the structure also provides for combination of bearings and its accessories, such as lock nuts, locking washers, etc.
. Equivalent bearings from different manufacturers are to be codified under one material code only.
. ISO designations, wherever applicable, shall be indicated.
. Wherever possible two or three sub-classifications of bearing shall be accommodated under one major classification to ensure better utilization of the scheme.
+
Eg.: AA 721 33 01 to AA 721 33 15 covers Single Row Needle and Single Thrust Ball Bearings and AA 721 33 16 onwards covers Double Row Needle And Single Thrust Ball Bearings.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Electrical & Electronic Components

[arabic]
. Material Code Numbers for Electrical and Electronic components shall be allotted under the main group 74.
. The Code structure will cover all Electrical and Electronic components used for production as well as maintenance. But components which are exclusively required for maintenance (such as motors for machine tools of BHEL factory, components of power house, sub-station, distribution network, etc.) will not be covered under group 74. This will be codified under Primary category 8.
. All components which are bought by supplying drawings and product specifications by BHEL shall be codified under Primary category 9.
. In the electronic components the values of resistance and capacitance shall be based on E 24 series as per IEC 63. The values not covered by E 24 series shall be codified at Plant / Product level.
. Spares/Accessories, wherever applicable for each component are also coded and they are allotted a specific block of numbers from 71 to 80 in the 8th and 9th digits in the 12-digit code structure. Spares/Accessories at Corporate level will be operated from 71 to 76 in ascending order and at plant level from 77 to 80 in descending order from 80.
+
For example:
+
AA7410171 represents spare bulbs in signal lamp assembly represented by AA74101 and AA 74 101 72 represents spare lenses in the same assembly.
+
A block of 81 to 99 in the 8^th^ and 9^th^ digits i.e. for specification details of a component will be reserved for plant level operation. These numbers will be operated in descending order from 99 to 81.
. A material code can have equivalents of supplier / manufacturer references.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Valves

[arabic]
. All standard pressure ratings and material of valves are pre-coded in pressure and material barrel (3 digits) from 001 to 600. For non-standard pressure ratings and material combinations numbers from 601 to 999 in the barrel.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Measuring and Control Devices

[arabic]
. Measuring and Control devices for pressure, temperature, level, speed, weight, time density etc. including penumatic, electrical / electronic controllers shall be covered under group '73' in Codification Structure.
. Ordinary valves used in the pipelines for flow regulation, stopping and safety relief purposes covering globe, gate, check, safety valves etc. which are generally classified as mechanical valves shall not be covered under this group.
. Items to National / Inter-national standards and supplier's catalogues shall be considered for codification. However, codification shall not be based on supplier's brand names or designations, but shall be based on BHEL Standards formulated by specifying the requirements. BHEL standards thus formulated shall be used as purchasing specifications for these items.

==== Guidelines for Codification of Welding Consumables

[arabic]
. As regards the Codification of Electrodes, efforts are made to codify them as per AWS grade or Specification and not as per Brand name. However, wherever it was not possible, the Brandwise codifications are done in few cases.
. For Electrodes, the sizes 2.5 mm, 3.15 mm and 5 mm have been codified as these sizes are widely used in various plants. However, it has been decided that Electrodes received in sizes 2.4 mm, 3.2 mm and 4.8 mm shall be codified as 2.5 mm, 3.15 mm and 5 mm respectively.
+
While in case of wires in continuous length/cut length the sizes of 2.4 mm, 3.2 mm, 4.8 mm and 6.3 mm have been codified.
+
However, wires in continuous length/cut length received in sizes 2.5 mm, 3.15 mm, and 5 mm are codified under the nearest sizes of 2.4 mm, 3.2 mm and 4.8 mm respectively.

=== Shapes

==== Shape Code Definitions for Ferrous Metals

===== Wire

Solid circular section that has been reduced by cold drawing through a die having diameters from 0.125 mm to 5mm, both inclusive.

===== Bar

Any solid section having diameter/greatest distance between parallel faces above 5mm

===== Shim

A flat product having thickness from 0.05 mm to 0.49mm both inclusive and widths up to and including 650 mm. shims to be codified on the basis of thickness.

===== Strip

A flat product having thickness from 0.50 mm to 4.99mm, both inclusive& Widths up to and including 650 mm in coil/flat form to be codified on the basis of width and thickness.

===== Sheet

A flat product having thickness from 0.50 mm to 4.99mm both inclusive& widths over 650mm.Sheet to be codified on the basis of thickness only.

===== Plate

A flat product of thickness 5 mm and above. Plates to be codified on the basis of thickness only.

===== Tubes

====== Seamless Tube

A hollow product of round cross-section having a continuous periphery produced by drawing or extrusion process. Tube to be codified on the basis of O.D and thickness.

====== Welded Tube

Tube formed from plate, sheet or strip with a longitudinal welded joint. Tube to be codified on the basis of O.D. and thickness.

===== Billet/Bloom

A cast solid product of specific size and shape intended for re-melting/forging of size 50 mm square and above.

==== Shape Codes for Non-Ferrous Metals

===== Wire

Solid circular section that has been reduced by cold drawing through a die to a diameter up to and including 6 mm.

===== Rod

A round solid section with diameter greater than 6 mm usually in straight lengths manufactured by rolling, drawing, extrusion or by a combination of these processes.

===== Bar

Any solid section other than round whose width or greatest distance between parallel faces is greater than 6 mm.

===== Foil

A rolled product of thickness up to and including 0.15 mm and width up to and including 800 mm, generally not cut to length, usually in coil form but may also be in flat or folded form.

===== Strip

A rolled product of thickness over 0.15 mm and up to and including 6 mm, and width up to and including 800 mm, generally not cut to length, usually in coil form but may also be in flat or folded form.

===== Sheet

A rolled product of thickness over 0.15 and up to and including 6 mm, and width over 800 mm supplied flat with sheared or slit edges.

===== Plate

A rolled product of thickness over 6 mm and width over 800 mm. supplied flat with sheared, milled or sawn edges and with less control of surface finish and tolerance which applies to sheet.

===== Tubes

====== Seamless Tube

A hollow product of round cross section having a continuous periphery produced by drawing or extrusion process.

====== Welded Tube

Tube formed from plate, sheet or strip with a longitudinal welded joint.

====== Drawn Tube

A hollow product of uniform wall thickness not produced by cold drawing.

====== Extruded Tube

A hollow extrusion of uniform wall thickness not produced by cold drawing.

===== Ingot

A cast solid product of specific size and shape (usually weighing between 9 to 16 kg) intended for remelting. It is usually notched to facilitate breaking in to smaller pieces.

===== Wire Bar

A horizontally or vertically cast product of approximately square cross section with sides and ends slightly tapered and weighing between 100 to 120 kg intended for the manufacture of wire rod.

===== Wire Rod

Rod like product of uniform cross section exceeding 6 mm obtained by casting and rolling or extrusion used as intermediate product for further processing, supplied in straight length or coil.

===== Forging

A shape produced by hammering or pressing usually when hot.

===== Stamping

A forging produced between open or closed dies.

===== Pressing

A shape produced between closed dies on a hydraulic or mechanical press.

=== Codification vs Quality

The specification parameters are built into the significant digits of the code number and hence it indicates the quality of the material. The indentor copies the code number along with the Standard Description of the material, the relevant Standard Number and grade.

The Code Number along with other details is carried over from the indent to the Material Purchase Requisition, Purchase order, Stores Issue Vouchers, Bin Card etc., keeping the identity of the material.

Thus, it ensures that the right quality material as per the requirement of the designer is procured, stocked and issued to the shop for the appropriate application.

=== Code Number as Inventory Control Number

In manufacturing industries, materials account for about 70% of the cost of the production. Modern industrial enterprises are giant establishments involving tremendous, capital investment. As a result of scientific inventory control techniques, if we can reduce the cost on materials by say 5%, it would result in substantial savings.

Increased Inventories would result in unnecessary block-up of capital and lead to more inventory carrying costs while insufficient inventories would create stock-outs, failure of attaining the production targets and delivery dates, which finally lead to loosing customer good will and the reputation of the organization.

Code Number will help to maintain the inventories at optimum level by exercising 'Variety Control'. All the new materials will be referred to standards section for code numbers. At these stages, the indents will be thoroughly scrutinized to see that whether any equivalent rationalized materials could be used instead of procuring new varieties. Discussions will be held by standards with concerned designer and other agencies. New varieties will be allowed when they are absolutely required. Code Number also enables to obtain classified computer out-puts to facilitate ABC analysis, fixing of minima and maxima levels to determine ordering quantities etc.

=== Usefulness of Code Number

Code Number facilitates the generation of the required computer outputs for analysis of data for various purposes:

==== Designs

[loweralpha]
. *Selection of Right Material* - The computerized code catalogue is used for indenting the rationalized materials.
. *Tendering & Estimation* - This is one of the critical functions of the designer and he is required to estimate the cost of the product as per customers specifications within a short time.

____
Code Number plays an important role in generating the computer outputs which are helpful in this connection.
____

[loweralpha]
. Project wise consumption of materials
. Work order-wise consumption of Material
. Work order - wise cost details

==== Material Control / Inventory Control

____
*Inventory Analysis and Control* — Computerised systems provides correct data on the consumption of stocks which can be further used for the following purposes:
____

[loweralpha]
. Inventory Analysis
. Maintaining stock levels
. Procurement planning

____
Computerized code catalogue which given an indication of rationalized / non-rationalized, stock / non-stock can be effectively utilized by material control to generate a computer output on non-rationalized code numbers for taking the necessary corrective actions.
____

[loweralpha, start=4]
. Consumption of non-rationalized materials for alternative application or by other plants of BHEL.
. Disposal of non-rationalized / non-moving material which cannot be used otherwise.

==== Stores

____
*Scientific Stocking* - Stocking of materials as per Code Number groups similar materials at one place, thereby eliminating duplicate stocking, reduction of floor space and inventory carrying costs. Since the issue and receipt of materials is based on Code Number, it facilitates even the unskilled workers to handle the stores. The indication regarding the correct and alternative store number given in the computerized material code catalogue helps to re-organize the store in case the same material is stocked in more than one stores.
____

==== Management Reporting

____
Generation of computerized information is one of the benefits of codification. Code Number is used in the computer systems for classification / sorting of data as per material category / group / shape / store-wise for generating quick and correct reports.

=== Systems Approach to Codification

Code Numbers cut across various functions in the Organization. Many persons deal with them in their daily activity. In an organization adopting computerized systems, it is essential to ensure the correctness of code numbers. In this connection, IPSC lays down the following Instructions:

*Indent:* It will be obligatory on the part of the various indentors to indicate code numbers in all their requisitions in addition to the description and specification of material by referring the codes catalogues.

*UMCD:* In the case of non-availability of code number, new code number can be generated in UMCD.

*Material Control:* In addition, Material Control shall check to ensure the correctness of code numbers in the Indents, Material Purchase Requisitions and Stores Receipt Vouchers. It is the particular responsibility of material control to see that no document is passed without the indication of code numbers.

*Purchase Department:* Purchase department shall be responsible to indicate code numbers as appearing in Purchase Requisitions on documents like Tender enquiry, comparative statement and Purchase Orders.

*Stores Department:* Stores department shall indicate code numbers from Purchase Order on Stores Receipt Voucher. They shall keep Bin cards in the sequence of code numbers.

*Finance:* The material accounts shall indicate code numbers in the material ledgers and keep the books in such a way as to identify the ledger sheets by referring material code numbers.

In spite of the above instructions, the following problems are faced in practice.

==== Indent Stage:

==== Some of the indents are released without Code Numbers in an urgency to procure the materials. Since the indents by-pass the UMCD, the following cases may arise:

[loweralpha]
. Procurement of a new variety which could perhaps be eliminated by utilizing another rationalized and stock material.
. Insufficient specification details which lead to procurement of inferior quality of material.
. Lack of specification details which poses problems for the inspector at the Inward inspection stage.

==== Lack of information whether an item is recurring / non-recurring, stock / non-stock and its annual consumption.

==== Inability of the indenter to provide complete specification details, particularly in the case of new projects and imported items. This leads to importing of the materials and components which could perhaps be procured indigenously as per relevant Indian Standards.

==== Acceptance of a deviation / amendment to the original specification without effecting the corresponding corrections to the Code Number. For example, Indenting of Black fasteners and amending the indent for plated fasteners at a later date without any correction to the Code Numbers would create problem of mix-up to the plated fasteners with un-plated one in the Stores.

==== The Code Number and the description may not tally. If the description is wrong, it leads to wrong procurement, whereas, wrong code leads to issue of wrong material to production shops / CDC.

==== Lack of clarity on the part of indenter whether the materials would be procured as an assembly / individual items, sometimes Code Numbers are indicated as per individual items whereas the material is received in assembled form vice versa is also possible.

==== Material Purchase Requisition (MPR) Stage:

____
Material Control used to process the indents without Code Numbers sometimes, due to which codification had to be done at SRV Stage. Hence the following difficulties are encountered.
____

[loweralpha]
. The very purpose of codification which is 'Inventory Control' is defeated since materials are already procured.
. Difficulty in inspecting the materials due to lack of correct and complete specification details.
. Inability of the Stores personnel to provide the necessary specification details to Standards Section at SRV Stage for the purpose of codification.

==== Purchase Stage

____
Amending the purchase orders as per the advice of indenters without correcting the relevant code numbers. This creates mix-up of Stocks of different specifications.
____

==== Systems Approach

____
The UMCD System has been implemented to facilitate the centralized search and generation of material codes across all units, operating at three levels from the indenter to the Plant Standards’ Committee Chairman (PSC) and the Central Authority.
____
